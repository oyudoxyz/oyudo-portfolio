ABOUT ME
I'm passionate about creating beautiful products that empower people.
Rooted in Architecture.
‍
My journey with architecture commenced in January 2017 at the University of Lagos, where my fascination with structural design ignited. Over the course of my studies, I honed a keen interest in the intricate art of frameworks, while also cultivating a knack for swiftly assimilating new concepts.

Driven by modularity and adaptability, I envision a dynamic built environment that evolves with lives and society, fueling my architectural goals.
This is my story — alongside some flicks from my recent trip to Ghana.
Transitioning to UX

During my time in school, I embarked on a graphic design journey, refining my skills and securing a product design internship. This marked my transition into the world of UX design, where learning and hands-on experience intertwined to shape my career path.
But, I wanted more.
‍
Though I loved the freedom of the academic design environment, I was dissatisfied with how slow the industry actually moved.

I wanted to push my design craft at a faster pace and have a positive impact on vastly more people.
In my spare time,
‍
I'm fully immersed in the realm of creators. You'll frequently find me engrossed in lively discussions about music, fashion, movies, and sharing laughter with those in my circle.

Beyond that, my interests span across enjoying anime, immersing myself in football matches as both a player and a spectator, and channeling my creativity into crafting videos. I'm also on a journey to learn Spanish on Duolingo.
‍

Thanks so much for stopping by!
Making it all happen.‍

To my advantage, I was able leverage a lot of the skills and design principles I had picked up during architecture school to greatly expedite my journey of self-learning UX Design.

I loved solving problems by making stuff, and really valued visual storytelling and paying meticulous attention to precision and craftsmanship.
